App Store Submission

Beyond the technical submission mechanics from Section 13, Apple requires a complete store listing and adherence to their review guidelines. Missing any of these is the most common reason first-time submissions get rejected — not code bugs.

Check off each item as you prepare your own submission
App Store readiness 0 / 7
Requirement Why Apple checks it
App icon (1024–1024) Used across the App Store and device home screens — must be a single, non-transparent image
Screenshots per device size Shown to users before download; must reflect the actual app, not mockups
Privacy policy URL Legally required if the app collects any user data, which most apps do
App Privacy "nutrition label" A declared summary of exactly what data your app collects and why
Demo account (if login required) Reviewers need to actually test past your login screen
The single most common first-time rejection reason Submitting an app that requires login without providing reviewer demo credentials in App Store Connect's review notes — reviewers cannot test what they can't get past your sign-in screen, and will reject on that basis alone.
Beginner mistakeUsing placeholder/lorem-ipsum screenshots or descriptions, which almost guarantees rejection for misrepresenting the app.
Intermediate mistakeUnder-declaring data collection in the App Privacy label, which can trigger a rejection or removal later if it's found to be inaccurate.

TOPIC 2 / 2

Play Store Submission

Google Play's requirements overlap with Apple's, but with a few distinctly Android/Play-specific steps of its own.

Check off each item as you prepare your own submission
Play Store readiness 0 / 7
Requirement Why Google checks it
Data safety form A structured, mandatory declaration of what data is collected, shared, and why — shown directly on your store listing
Content rating questionnaire Determines the age rating shown to users, based on your answers about app content
Feature graphic (1024–500) The banner image shown at the top of your Play Store listing
Target API level compliance Google requires targeting a recent Android API level, updated roughly yearly
A Play-specific trap: target API level Unlike Apple's more qualitative review, Google enforces a hard, mechanical requirement: your app must target a sufficiently recent Android API level or it will be rejected outright, regardless of how well it otherwise works — this deadline shifts roughly annually, so check current requirements before every major release.
Beginner mistakeFilling out the Data Safety form inaccurately relative to what the app's actual code/dependencies collect (including third-party SDKs) — this is checked and can be flagged.
Intermediate mistakeNot revisiting the target API level requirement on a yearly cadence, leading to a scramble when an existing app suddenly can't be updated.
